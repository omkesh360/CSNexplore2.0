The Best Street Food Spots in Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar

T

CSNExplore Team

March 6, 2026

Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar — once famously known as Aurangabad — is a city that wears its history in stunning monuments, but its true soul lives on its streets. The aroma of sizzling masalas, the clatter of iron pans, and the colour of freshly prepared chutneys make street food here not just a meal, but an experience. Whether you're a first-time visitor or a local veteran, these spots will leave your taste buds craving more.

1. Gulmandi Chowk — The Heartbeat of Street Food

📍 Gulmandi Square, Old City
Best for: Naan Qalia, Shikampuri Kebabs, Haleem

No street food tour of CSN is complete without a pilgrimage to Gulmandi. This buzzing intersection in the old city is ground zero for authentic Naan Qalia — a slow-cooked mutton gravy served with flaky naan straight off the tandoor. Arrive hungry, bring cash, and prepare for a queue. The chaos is part of the charm.

The nearby Shikampuri Kebab stalls are equally legendary. These shallow-fried patties stuffed with minced lamb and fragrant spices are a culinary heirloom passed down through generations of Hyderabadi-influenced families who settled in the city centuries ago.

2. Misal Pav Trail — The Maharashtrian Staple

📍 Nirala Bazar & Samarth Nagar
Best for: Spicy Usal Misal, Kolhapuri Misal, Buttery Pav

Misal Pav is Maharashtra's quintessential breakfast, and CSN takes it seriously. The base is a fiery sprouted moth-bean curry (usal) topped with crunchy farsan, diced onions, fresh coriander, and a squeeze of lime. Locals swear by the Kolhapuri variant — red-hot and unapologetically spicy — served with thick, buttery pav rolls.

💡 Pro Tip
Ask for "teekha" for extra heat or "medium" if you prefer something more balanced. Pair it with a glass of chilled lassi from the stall next door — you'll need it!

3. Chaat Paradise near Kranti Chowk

📍 Kranti Chowk, Central CSN
Best for: Pani Puri, Dahi Puri, Sev Puri, Bhel Puri

The chaat stalls clustered around Kranti Chowk erupt to life every evening as office workers and school children gather for their daily fix of Pani Puri. The tamarind-sweet water is balanced perfectly with spicy mint water, and the crispy puris explode with flavour at first bite.

Dahi Puri — layered with creamy yoghurt, sev, and date-tamarind chutney — is the gentler, more indulgent cousin of Pani Puri. Try both. In fact, try everything.

4. Pav Bhaji Corner at Mondha

📍 Mondha Bus Stand Area
Best for: Butter-Loaded Pav Bhaji, Masala Pav

The Pav Bhaji here is cooked on a wide iron tawa with alarming amounts of butter — and it is absolutely glorious. The vegetable mash is spiced beautifully and finished with a dollop of fresh butter that melts into the bhaji before your eyes. The charred pav rolls fried on the same tawa soak up the gravy perfectly.

Masala Pav — where the pav itself is tossed in the bhaji mixture on the tawa — is a popular variation that locals order alongside the main dish.

5. Saoji Food — A Regional Specialty

📍 Various locations across the city
Best for: Saoji Mutton, Saoji Chicken Curry

While technically not street food, Saoji cuisine from the Vidarbha region has street-stall versions sprinkled across CSN. Saoji curries are distinct for their intense, dark masala made from over 30 spices that result in a deeply layered, fiery flavour profile. It's Vidarbha's answer to Kolhapuri spice, and it commands serious respect.

Look for small eateries with handpainted signs that say "Saoji" — these are usually family-run gems with recipes guarded like state secrets.

6. Evening Bhutta (Roasted Corn) — Casual & Classic

As the sun dips behind the Daulatabad Fort on the horizon, vendors fire up coal grills across the city to roast corn on the cob. Rubbed with lime, rock salt, and red chilli powder, the humble bhutta becomes something magical in the cool evening air. Pick one up near the Bibi Ka Maqbara gardens for the perfect sunset snack.

Best Times to Eat Street Food in CSN

  • Morning (7–10 AM): Misal Pav, Poha, Kande Pohe
  • Midday (12–2 PM): Naan Qalia, Kebabs, Thali places near Gulmandi
  • Evening (5–9 PM): Chaat, Pav Bhaji, Bhutta, Vada Pav
  • Night (9 PM–12 AM): Biryani stalls, Seekh Kebabs near old city

Getting Around the Food Trail

Most of these spots are best explored on foot or by auto-rickshaw. If you're visiting multiple areas, consider renting a bike from our rentals for the day. CSN's old city roads can be narrow, and two wheels give you the freedom to chase aromas down every lane.

🌟 Final Verdict
Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar's street food scene is a living, breathing celebration of its multicultural heritage — Mughal, Maratha, and everything in between. Come hungry, eat fearlessly, and leave with stories. Your stomach will thank you.
